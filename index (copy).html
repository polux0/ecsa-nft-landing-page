Key Principles

Universal Issuance Rights

Every agent possesses the authority to issue assets, including money and equity, that are guided by distinct logics. There are no central issuers, ledgers, or privileged financial agencies. Accountability rests with the distributed network itself.

Economic-organizational expression

The power to issue assets becomes a catalyst for economic communication, coordination, and the organization of postcapitalist value forms and alternative modes of value calculation. We move from from social media to economic media.

Mutually-regulated issuance

Issuance is not subject to centralized regulation. Instead, agents within the network mutually regulate this process, leveraging their collective information and computational capacities.

A DIFFERENT ECONOMIC NETWORKING PARADIGM

Reciprocal stakeholding serves as our primary networking primitive. Agents establish peer to peer economic links through reciprocal stakeholding, credit, and performance relationships.

RISKING TOGETHER

Every participant shares both the downside and upside of their economic performances. Everyone is directly or transitively invested in each other, so the network as a whole is invested in every agent.

ANYTHING CAN BE ENCODED AS AN ECONOMIC PERFORMANCE

Performance enables the expression and participation in new value forms, recognizing, organizing, and valuing externalities within the economic network such as clean air, biosphere health, knowledge creation, and education.

BEYOND PROFIT AS THE SOLE RECOGNIZED SURPLUS

Agents have the freedom to choose how they distribute the value they create, unrestricted by profit calculations. They can utilize performance indexes as the measurements that guide their economic decisions.

INTRODUCING THE ECONOMIC NETWORKS

The Economic Space Agency (ECSA) is an organisation deeply rooted in postcapitalist economic principles. It champions a new approach to economics that emphasises collaboration, shared risk-taking, and collective wealth redefinition and distribution. ECSA goes beyond theory, actively proposing and experimenting with new protocols, accounting practices, and financial instruments, the first of which is upcoming.
